# Larderlab — Affiliate Partner Reference

The affiliate brands Larderlab will review across Wave 1 comparison posts, the networks that carry them, and the application strategy.

**Last updated:** April 2026. Rates and network coverage drift — verify before onboarding. Per CLAUDE.md + the brand book §12, affiliate commissions never influence rankings.

---

## 1. Affiliate networks

| Network | Covers | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Amazon Associates** | Most supplements, bulk protein, meal prep containers, kitchen tools | 1-4.5%. Wide reach, weak rates. Apply after 10+ posts live. |
| **ShareASale** | Mid-market supplement brands, meal delivery, cookware | 8-15%. Merchant approval per brand. |
| **Impact.com** | Premium DTC (Thorne, Ritual, Athletic Greens/AG1, Momentous, Legion), meal delivery (Factor, CookUnity) | 10-20%. Per-merchant approval. |
| **Awin** | European supplement brands + some US crossover | Niche in our space. |
| **Rakuten** | Williams-Sonoma, Sur La Table (for kitchen storage / tools) | Mainstream retailer crossover. |
| **AvantLink** | Outdoor / kitchen / sustainability brands — some storage + gear brands here | Worth joining for the niche coverage. |
| **Direct affiliate programs** | Transparent Labs, Bulk Supplements, Legion, Thrive Market, Costco Affiliate (limited), Cronometer, MacroFactor | Usually 10-20% direct. Higher admin. |
| **Thrive Market partner program** | Bulk pantry + supplement crossover | Good match for the pantry-systems hub |

**Application order:**
1. Month 1: nothing (zero affiliate links, build site + content first)
2. Month 2 (10+ posts live): Amazon Associates, ShareASale network + top 10 merchants, Impact network
3. Month 3 (20+ posts): direct programs (Transparent Labs, Bulk Supplements, Legion, Thrive Market), AvantLink, Rakuten
4. Month 4+: evaluate performance per merchant, prune underperformers, double down on top 5

## 7. Revenue model — rough Wave 1 math

Assumptions:
- 15,000 monthly sessions by month 6
- 3% click-through to affiliate link (higher than plasticfreelab because calculators drive high-intent clicks to supplement comparisons)
- 3% conversion on clicks
- $30 blended commission per conversion (weighted toward supplements + meal delivery)

**15,000 × 0.03 × 0.03 × $30 = $405/month at month 6.**

Realistic target at 50k sessions by month 12: **$3,500-5,500/month**.

Once the Macro Calculator gets picked up by even one Reddit thread or one newsletter (Attia's "Proof," Momentous's "Performance Outlook," similar), a ranking bump + high-intent-traffic flywheel kicks in. Calculators are the moat.

## 8. Compliance checklist per affiliate page

Every page containing an affiliate link must have (site-spec §4 enforces these):

- [ ] FTC disclosure above the fold via `<AffiliateDisclosure>` component
- [ ] `rel="sponsored nofollow noopener"` on every affiliate `<a>` via `<AffiliateLink>`
- [ ] Methodology section on comparison pages
- [ ] `<PriceAsOf>` component wrapping every product price, dated
- [ ] "Our pick" clearly labeled with the criterion
- [ ] At least one non-affiliate alternative mentioned where it's genuinely better
- [ ] Sources section with DOIs or URLs
- [ ] Third-party testing status clearly shown per supplement

## 10. What this document is not

This is not a ranking. Actual ranks per comparison post come from the brief generator + editorial review + third-party testing data + real-world testing. This is the candidate list of **brands we'd evaluate for inclusion**.

When a comparison post goes through the pipeline (per `docs/larderlab-sample-briefs.md`), the draft generator pulls from this list to populate the product candidates — but the winner and the order come from the brief + editorial process.
